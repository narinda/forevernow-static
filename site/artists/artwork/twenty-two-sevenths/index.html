Twenty Two Sevenths

George Carver

What can we communicate to intelligent life about ourselves and our place in the Universe in one short minute? “Twenty Two Sevenths” is a musical and visual expression of Pi to the first two hundred eighty eight digits. This is a means to say, we come from a base twelve planet whose life is illuminated by these colors of light, and that we can embrace a means of expression that includes irrational numbers.

Musician George Carver currently resides in Austin, Texas. He was born and raised in Buffalo, New York, inspired equally by futurist R. Buckminster Fuller and bluesman Muddy Waters. As a young man he took up residence in New York City working as a composer, producer and engineer with many artists from modern jazz improviser Cooper-Moore, shakuhachi master James Nyoraku, and Nigerian drummer Babatunde Olatunji. Moving to Austin in 1991, he took up the eight string steel guitar, immersing himself in Texas Swing, using the energetic, feel-good sound as a counterpoint to the more darkly humorous music he currently writes and performs.
