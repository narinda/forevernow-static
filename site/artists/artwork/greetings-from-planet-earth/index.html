Greetings from Planet Earth

Alan Grace

A piece of pro-earth propaganda from America in the 1950s.

Alan Grace has been working in the theatre since he was a little boy. He graduated for AC Arts (Acting) in 2007, and was Dramaturge/Assistant Director for floogle’s Ollie and the Minotaur for Fringe 2008. In 2009 with seven other graduates of AC Arts he established Adelaide Duende Collective. Their inaugural production, The Embryos, written by Alan, was produced in Fringe 2010, and received a nomination for the Jill Blewett’s Playwright’s Award. He performed with Splash in Mr Badger, What Big Teeth You Have, and The Book Safari. In 2010, for Duende he directed Theatre Ltd, wrote Pinter in the Pub for Urban Myth, and participated in Vitalstatistix’ Adhocracy. His play No Hello premiered in Fringe ‘11, garnering a Inspace Development Nomination. In May 2011 he conceived The Play-Off Challenge and directed Bigger than Beanbag for it. In November Duende premiered Slicing and Dicing!!! directed by Peter Dunn. In 2012, Duende have premiered German Diary and Truth Teller – which he also directed. This year he will write for Urban Myth with No Strings Attached, and at AC Arts where second years will perform Hysterical Blindness directed by David Mealor. He also stars in the short film Steak Knife, winner of Best Comedy at the SA Screen Awards this year. He recently produced the Second Play-Off Challenge and directed the winning play Red Ink by Duncan Graham, and participated in Adhocracy 2012 with the resident artists, Parachutes for Ladies. His play sex play will premiere as part of the Blackbox season later this year.
