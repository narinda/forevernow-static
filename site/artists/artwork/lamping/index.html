Lamping

conor o grady

Out of the darkness, the nocturnal abyss. A hare caught in the strobe light. Followed closely by the savage threat of a Grey Hounds teeth. The fear of the unknown and the realisation that we are but a mere speck on the surface of the universe.

Conor O’ Grady’ work focuses on the complexity involved in the representation of systemic behaviour within society, namely that representation of change or flux within norms of behaviour. Especially in the emergence and classification of sub-groups which exist both inside and outside of larger society. The practice outlined here, hopes to reflect society’s ever changing relationship with, among others, technological systems. In particular the internet and the exchange in methods of cultural and artistic interaction: How this relationship effects the production and consumption of that culture. The advancement of Wi-Fi technology and cameras built into mobile phones, means that change can be viewed within the same instant with which it occurs, changing the negotiation between viewer and systems created to control what is viewed.
