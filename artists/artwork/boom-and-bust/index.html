Boom and Bust

Christian Nicolay & Ya-chu Kang

Boom and Bust is part of an ongoing body of work examining the ebb and flow of cycles and the balance of opposing forces. Working with participants from many different places we were able to discuss ongoing global issues that we all share. The simple action of popping balloons set the wheels in motion to use art as a platform for critical discourse and thus a vehicle to progress our understanding of one another. Boom and Bust depicts a world in which rational and irrational co-exist situated between absurdity and reality, familiar and unfamiliar, the vulnerable and invulnerable. What connects us all is our humanness, where a smile or laugh can break down barriers simultaneously erecting building blocks for progress and compassion.

Christian Nicolay and Ya-chu Kang have been collaborating since 2010, based between Vancouver Canada and Taipei Taiwan. They work with sculpture, installation, video, sound recording, performance and site-specific projects often dealing with the shifting polarities between Western and Eastern cultural landscapes which they define as “portable walls”. Fascinated with rituals and superstitions from around the world their research deals with cultural divisions and relationships using art to effectively break down borders and boundaries blurring the lines between art and the everyday.
