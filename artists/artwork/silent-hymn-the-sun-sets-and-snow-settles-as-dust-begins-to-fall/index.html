Silent Hymn (the sun sets and snow settles as dust begins to fall)

jacobus capone

The magnitude and mystery of our existence is sometimes humbly encapsulated in the most insignificant or slightest of instances and things we witness. For one or the other of us sometimes the sensation of these scenarios, in their very existence evoke a fleeting importance that words fail at translating, they are simply lived intensely or they are not. It could very well be that these occurrences have no practical importance and because of this are liberated from any associations with reality. Our sense of wonderment in such moments is valuable and unique insofar as the instance itself speaking of the unreal, and unfathomable. And in our contemplating of these minor and futile things an interlude ultimately emerges that echo’s the mystery of our own existence. All of this could be everything, mean everything, or simply be nothing. But for now as the sunsets and the snow settles, dust begins to fall.

Working between durational performance, video installation, painting and site specifically Jacobus’s work has a constant ephemeral quality where the desire and constant effort to integrate all action into the wholeness of one lived experience takes precedence.
